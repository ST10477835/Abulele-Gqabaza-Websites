About Me

My name is Abulele Gqabaza, and I am a first-year computer science student at IIE Varsity College in Cape Town. I completed both my primary and high school education at Ashbury College, where I consistently performed at the top of my class, earning top achiever awards in various subjects and ranking first among the top five students.

I am passionate about continuous learning and deeply resonate with Albert Einstein’s words: “Wisdom is not a product of schooling but of the lifelong attempt to acquire it.” This quote reflects my dedication to the pursuit of knowledge, which is one of the many reasons I was drawn to the field of computer science. I am a driven and hardworking individual, eager to grow, innovate, and contribute meaningfully in the tech world.

The software development journey is full of constant challenges and problems that require creative thinking and perseverance—and that’s exactly why I enjoy it so much. From the initial frustration and confusion of encountering a bug to the euphoric sense of accomplishment when I finally solve it, each moment fuels my passion for this field and strengthens my commitment to it.

This mindset extends into my personal life as well. I enjoy activities like chess, drawing, writing, and gaming—all of which involve problem-solving, strategy, and creativity.
